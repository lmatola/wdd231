About Us

The country is known for producing some of the best dishes in Africa, representing a true fusion of Portuguese, Arab and African influences. It is also famous for its seafood, including prawns, lobsters, crabs and squid, which are highly appreciated by visitors. Local dishes along the coast have a charm that makes them unique and even more appetizing compared to food from neighboring countries, when ingredients such as coconut and piri-piri are used.

Upshwa or xima is one of the favorite side dishes and is made from corn or cassava flour and served with fish, meat or vegetables. One of Mozambique's most famous specialties is mathapa, a dish made from cassava leaves cooked in peanut sauce and usually prepared with crab or shrimp.



Most restaurants along the coast cook seafood at affordable prices, especially lobster, crayfish, grilled prawns and squid that can be stuffed, grilled or fried. Portions are very well served in this restaurant, so it is possible to have a meal even with starters alone! Accompany the garlic shrimp with garlic butter bread, lobster tapas, crayfish tails, crayfish samosas, grandmother's meatballs, lobster patties, ceviche, goat cheese wrapped in puff pastry, beef fillet sliced ​​in marrow bone and toast, tapas of Atlantic horse mackerel and Indian shrimp with cornbread, smoked sardines with cornbread.

Some examples of meat dishes: beef fillet, picanha, meat kebab, lamb chop, grilled chicken and grilled duck breast. Seafood includes shrimp of all sizes, grilled crayfish and lobster. Fish dishes include cod or grouper fillet, both steamed, grilled Scottish salmon fillet, tartare and tuna fillet, fish with seaweed, salted fish and grilled squid, for example.
